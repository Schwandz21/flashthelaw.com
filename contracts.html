The Big Picture

Most Contracts questions can be organized around one simple path: formation, enforceability, terms, performance, breach, and remedies.

Master Checklist

  • Formation: Was there offer, acceptance, and consideration?
  • Governing law: Common law for services/real estate; UCC Article 2 for sale of goods.
  • Defenses: Capacity, duress, undue influence, mistake, misrepresentation, illegality, unconscionability, statute of frauds.
  • Terms: Identify express terms, implied terms, gap fillers, modifications, and conditions.
  • Performance: Did the parties perform? Was performance excused?
  • Breach: Material breach, minor breach, anticipatory repudiation, or perfect tender issue?
  • Remedies: Expectation, reliance, restitution, specific performance, liquidated damages, mitigation.

Issue Spotting Triggers

When you see these facts, your brain should immediately jump to the related doctrine.

“I promise to give…”

Gift promise. Watch for lack of consideration unless reliance creates promissory estoppel.

Changed terms

Modification issue. Common law generally needs consideration; UCC needs good faith.

Oral deal

Statute of Frauds issue. Ask whether the contract type requires a writing.

Goods + merchants

UCC Article 2. Watch battle of the forms, firm offers, perfect tender, and warranties.

“Time is of the essence”

Condition/performance issue. Timeliness may become central to breach analysis.

One party quits early

Anticipatory repudiation or material breach. Consider adequate assurances and remedies.

Core Rule Statements

These are short, usable rule statements. On essays, state the rule, apply the facts, and move quickly.

Offer: An offer is a manifestation of willingness to enter into a bargain that creates the power of acceptance in another person.

Acceptance: Acceptance is an objective manifestation of assent to the terms of the offer, made in the manner invited or required by the offer.

Consideration: Consideration requires a bargained-for exchange where each party gives or promises something of legal value.

Promissory Estoppel: A promise may be enforceable without consideration when the promisor should reasonably expect reliance, the promisee does rely, and injustice can be avoided only by enforcement.

Statute of Frauds: Certain contracts must be evidenced by a writing signed by the party to be charged, including contracts for land, suretyship, marriage consideration, goods of $500 or more, and contracts not performable within one year.

Material Breach: A material breach substantially defeats the purpose of the contract and may excuse the nonbreaching party’s remaining performance.

Expectation Damages: Expectation damages seek to put the nonbreaching party in the position they would have occupied had the contract been performed.

Flashcards

Quick active-recall prompts. Read the question, answer it from memory, then check yourself.

Formation

What are the three basic requirements for contract formation?

Offer, acceptance, and consideration.
Consideration

Is a mere gift promise usually enforceable?

Usually no, because there is no bargained-for exchange. Check for reliance/promissory estoppel.
UCC

What law governs contracts for the sale of goods?

UCC Article 2 governs contracts for the sale of goods.
Remedies

What is the goal of expectation damages?

To put the nonbreaching party in the position they would have been in if the contract had been performed.
Defenses

What should you think when a contract is oral?

Statute of Frauds. Ask whether the agreement is a type that requires a writing.
Breach

What is anticipatory repudiation?

A clear statement or act showing that a party will not perform before performance is due.

Common MBE Traps

Contracts questions love small distinctions. These are the traps to watch for.

Trap List

  • Past consideration: Usually not valid consideration.
  • Advertisements: Usually invitations to deal, not offers, unless specific and limiting.
  • Silence: Usually not acceptance, unless circumstances make it reasonable.
  • Mailbox rule: Acceptance generally effective upon dispatch, but watch exceptions.
  • Option contracts: Acceptance effective upon receipt, not dispatch.
  • UCC firm offer: Merchant’s signed promise to keep offer open can be binding without consideration.
  • Perfect tender: UCC buyer may reject goods if they fail in any respect, subject to cure and exceptions.
  • Specific performance: More likely for unique goods or land; usually not for ordinary goods or personal services.

Essay Approach

When a Contracts essay appears, keep your structure simple and visible.

Use this essay order

  • Step 1: Identify the governing law: common law or UCC.
  • Step 2: Discuss formation: offer, acceptance, consideration.
  • Step 3: Discuss enforceability and defenses.
  • Step 4: Identify the relevant terms and any conditions.
  • Step 5: Analyze performance, breach, and excuses.
  • Step 6: Finish with remedies.
